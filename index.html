Who I Am

About Me

I'm a Mechanical Engineering student who loves bridging the gap between physical design and real engineering problems. Whether I'm running stress simulations or modelling enclosures in SolidWorks, I approach every problem the same way break it down, understand the fundamentals, and build something that actually works.

I prototype fast, test ruthlessly, and iterate until it works. Give me a weird constraint and I'll build something real around it: gear-driven launchers, wind turbines from air purifier exhaust, electromechanical fighting games. If it spins, deflects, or generates power, I want to understand why and then make it better.

Currently looking for co-op opportunities where I can learn fast, contribute meaningfully, and work with people who care about what they build.

What I've Built

Featured Projects

Full-Stack Python OpenTripPlanner GTFS
FuturTransit

A full-stack Toronto transit planner where users calculate real routes and toggle future infrastructure proposals like the Ontario Line and Yonge North Extension to see exactly how their commute changes. Built to advocate for transit by making proposals tangible. Integrated static GTFS data into OpenTripPlanner 2 with a FastAPI middleware layer and a React/Leaflet frontend. Generated synthetic GTFS feeds for 9 proposed lines using official Metrolinx data.

SolidWorks CAD Fluid Dynamics FEA Prototyping
Beyblade Assembly & Launcher

Designed a full Beyblade assembly and custom launcher in SolidWorks, inspired by the physics of spinning tops. Integrated a fan blade for fluid dynamics, engineered mass distribution across the disc for gyroscopic stability, and built a gear-driven launcher subassembly to maximise rotational energy at launch. All parts custom CAD except standalone gears.

Electromechanical SolidWorks Laser Cutting 3D Printing
Battle Hams

Two-player electromechanical fighting game with hamster-themed robotic characters that battle to knock off each other's headpiece. Designed and built the movement system using DC motors with H-bridge circuits for lateral movement and a pulley-driven mechanism for strikes. Solely responsible for the arena, modelled in SolidWorks and AutoCAD, fabricated via laser-cut acrylic, wood, and 3D printed PLA. Iterated from cardboard prototype to fully functional game.

Aerodynamics SolidWorks 3D Printing Electronics
Mini HAWT Wind Turbine In Progress

A small-scale horizontal axis wind turbine designed to sit on top of an air purifier, harvesting exhaust airflow to power a capybara-themed LED night light. Selected the E61 airfoil via XFoil polar analysis at Re 5,000, optimized 5-blade geometry using QBlade BEM simulation, and designed the full electrical system: stepper motor generator, dual bridge rectifiers, and a boost converter, targeting 0.14W output at 994 RPM. Fabricated with FDM PLA blades, SLA flexible motor damper, and a laser-cut wood and acrylic enclosure.
